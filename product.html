Before a plant walk

The questions plant teams ask first.

Straight answers on performance, integration, downtime, and support. Final acceptance criteria are set around your line and your product.

Our position We prove it on your own birds.
ProofHow accurate is it?
Representative testing has produced a false-positive rate below 1.5%. The result that matters is yours, so we bench-test your birds and return the grading data before you commit.
Live deploymentCan we speak with a current customer?
The system is running in live production. That customer is protected by NDA, so we do not name them or arrange a reference call. Instead, we demonstrate performance on your own product.
Line fitWill it work with our equipment?
The controller is designed to exchange simple signals with plant PLCs over Ethernet/IP and drive existing A, B, and C diverters. A line survey confirms the exact interface before the quotation is finalized.
InstallationHow much downtime should we expect?
We build and factory-test the system first. Ceiling mounting and line connection can be scheduled after hours or over a weekend. The unit requires 110V power and compressed air, followed by calibration and joint validation before go-live.
MaintenanceWho keeps it running?
Your team can handle routine care using the PM sheets and training we provide. The main tasks are keeping the camera glass clean and the computer cooled. One year of parts and labor is included, with service and extended terms available.
The distinctionIs this another weight grader?
No. Weight grading supports sizing and batching. PucPucChicken makes the quality condition call, assigning A, B, or C. It operates as a quality layer alongside your existing weight-grading process.
